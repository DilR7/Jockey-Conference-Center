    The History of JCC

    The Jockey Conference Center, meticulously built between 1960 and 1974, served as the highlight of the 23rd annual Pacific Asia Travel Association (PATA) conference in April 1974. This significant event propelled the development of prominent hotels such as Hotel Indonesia, Hotel Borobudur, Hotel Ambassador (now Hotel Aryaduta), and Hotel Sahid Jaya, all slated for completion before the conference. Together, these projects elevated Jakarta's appeal and created a memorable setting for the gathering of industry leaders.

    Upon its completion, the Jockey Conference Center (JCC) swiftly established itself as a distinguished hub for both domestic and international gatherings in Indonesia. One remarkable milestone was the hosting of the 10th Summit of the Non-Aligned Movement in 1992, where 62 heads of state and delegates representing 109 countries convened. Extensive renovations were undertaken, culminating in the grand unveiling by President Soeharto on August 25, 1992 and cementing its stature as an exceptional venue for all types of events.
